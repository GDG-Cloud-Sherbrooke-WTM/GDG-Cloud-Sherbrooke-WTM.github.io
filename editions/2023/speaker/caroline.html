Caroline Fortier

Girls don’t code – Addressing the bias in tech

Bio

Caroline has been working in Computer Science for over 20 years, first as a Solution Designer, Developer and Integrator in a Global Expertise Center in Telecommunications R&D. She earned her PSM in 2012, has implemented Agility for many clients, and has always valued delivering what provides the most business value to her clients in multiple industries. She has worked in many countries of Asia, Europe, Africa and North America and has managed many multi-site and multi-cultural teams with a lot of enthusiasm. She has now returned to her hometown and teaches Computer Science in a local College, preparing the next generation for the challenges that are awaiting them.

Summary

Addressing why women do not pursue careers in IT and why they should. Hopefully presenting a positive role model for young women whilst addressing the many bias that do not make our journey a easy one.
