11:00am Empowering Women in EdTech: The Importance of Female Leadership and Diversity
Laura Sánchez Fernández (Spotify Educational Podcast "Desde tus zapatos")
In this presentation, we will explore the significance of fostering female leadership and diversity in the fast-growing field of EdTech. Despite advancements in technology and education, women and other underrepresented groups continue to face significant challenges in the field. This presentation will address these challenges and emphasize the need for increased representation and involvement of women in STEM careers and educational entrepreneurship. The speaker, drawing on their personal experiences as a mathematician, teacher, and EdTech entrepreneur, will share their insights and provide examples that illustrate the impact of women's leadership and diversity in the industry. Attendees will gain a comprehensive understanding of the current landscape of EdTech, the challenges faced by women and underrepresented groups, and the opportunities for them to make a difference. At the end of the presentation, attendees will leave feeling inspired and equipped with the knowledge and resources necessary to pursue a career in EdTech and play an active role in promoting diversity and inclusiveness in the field.
11:30pm Preserving Diversity in Language Technologies
Bonaventure F. P. Dossou (PhD student, McGill-MILA)
Language technologies are inherent in human daily lives. Recent advances in AI and Natural Language Processing technologies showed how powerful computers could be useful to humans, especially as a means of communication. Unfortunately, existing systems are biased (gender, and race), and barely integrate low-resourced languages; while becoming more and more computationally expensive. In this presentation, as a part of his work, Bonaventure will share with us his experience working on methods leveraging how we can build efficient language technologies without relying on huge amounts of data, and computation, while also mitigating bias in language representation.

2:30pm 3 Good Night Stories in Bioinformatics for Rebel Girls
Alitzel Lopez Sanchez (University of Sherbrooke)
Bioinformatics is a multidisciplinary area that applies computer science and mathematics tools to study biological phenomena such as evolution. Thus, developing a project in this area requires an understanding of two scientific domains that seem far apart. In this presentation, I will share three stories about projects in this area which would have never been possible without the rebel spirit of the women involved. No prior knowledge of biology, computer science or mathematics is required.

3:30pm Fabrication de caloducs polymériques pour la gestion thermique de batteries dans les voitures éléctrique
Doriane Ibtissam Hassaine Daoudji (University of Sherbrooke)
Le maintien de la température d’opération des appareils électroniques dans une plage optimale est nécessaire. Particulièrement, nous visons à adresser cette problématique pour des dispositifs à faible flux de chaleur et à large surface comme les batteries lithium-ion dans les voitures éléctriques. Nous introduisons le caloduc comme une solution en gestion thermique pour ces applications. Notre objectif est de designer et de fabriquer cet échangeur de chaleur avec une structure poreuse optimisée pour augmenter sa capacité de transfert thermique. Les caloducs sont généralement fabriqués à partir de cuivre ou d’aluminium. Cependant, la rigidité, la densité et le coût des métaux font que le caloduc métallique n’est pas une bonne alternative en gestion thermique pour tous type d’applications. Par conséquent, nous introduisons le caloduc polymérique comme le mode de gestion thermique pour ces applications à large surface et à faible flux de chaleur.
